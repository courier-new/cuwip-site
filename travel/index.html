Support for Travel

Accommodation and meals will be provided for all attendees for the duration of the conference. However, APS CUWiP requests that participants ask their departments for travel funding. Please refer to this page when planning your means of transportation. Additional travel grants are available for those whose departments cannot cover full costs of the conference. If you have questions about this process or are unsure who to direct questions to within your department, please contact us.

General

The conference will be spread across the three colleges of Harvey Mudd, Pomona, and Cal Poly Pomona with one day per institution. Participants will stay at the DoubleTree Hotel in Claremont. Shuttles will take students to and from the hotel to Cal Poly Pomona on Sunday. We encourage local participants to travel to Claremont by car, bus, Metrolink, or train.

By Car

Carpooling is strongly encouraged. We will provide a list of all registered students, emails, and institutions after registration closes to help facilitate this process. Our Facebook is also available to help you connect with other conference participants. We advise attendees who are driving to first check in at DoubleTree, where parking is readily available in the hotel lot. DoubleTree's address is as follows:

DoubleTree by Hilton
555 W Foothill Blvd
Claremont, CA 91711

Can I apply?

Bear in mind that the Western Regional conference is intended to serve applicants from California (south of San Jose), Alaska, and Hawaii. Students from other locations are encouraged to check the map and apply to the conference site geographically closest to them. While the conference targets undergraduate women and underrepresented genders passionate about physics and astronomy, anyone seriously interested in attending may apply. For other questions about applicant eligibility, visit our FAQ.

Will I be admitted?

We anticipate that CUWiP@HMC+POM+CPP will be oversubscribed. Therefore, it is crucial to complete all parts of the application and to write a thoughtful Applicant Statement that conveys how this conference is meaningful for your personal and professional growth. In the event that the conference is oversubscribed, priority will be given to applicants who are:

  • Undergraduate physics majors
  • Community college students planning to transfer to a physics program at a four year institution
  • New to conferences, and specifically APS CUWiP
  • Applying to the conference that is geographically closest to their expected location January 12th to 14th
